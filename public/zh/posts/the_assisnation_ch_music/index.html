梦里水乐

2025年10月3日 · 4 分钟 | 语言:
  • English
  • 简体中文

即便不解一字，当你聆听江珊的《梦里水乡》，思绪亦能挣脱束缚，自由徜徉。乐声将你引向山间的村落，河水如齐腰襦裙般环抱山峦。你能闻到乡村未铺砌道路上湿润的泥土气息，能看见足迹封存于小径记忆中的往昔，能望见炊烟从旧厨房的屋顶袅袅升起，宛如中国的水袖舞。你能看见晨雾如轻纱，环绕在山峦的颈项。

竹笛之音，自中国乡村的上空扬起，将你推向太阳。河面上阳光的金色倒影，让你的双眼充满奇妙的幸福。而后，其他鸟鸣伴着古筝的声响而来，以一种柔滑的愉悦、一种纯粹天然的欢欣，轻颤你的灵魂。

那归鸟于日落前返巢的笃定，那母亲迎接久别孩儿的喜悦。

你的心神可以描绘出一位中国古画大师手中的笔，正在挥毫水墨。你随那支笔轻柔浮动，在最后一笔、最后一划中，感受画卷圆满的收梢。

这便是中国美学的灵魂，只不过它以音声呈现。中国音乐与中国水墨画并无二致。若一首中国音乐无法在你心中唤起这般感受，无法勾勒出园林、溪流、山岳、落日、浮云……即自然本身的美学意境，那它便不是中国音乐。那是舶来之物。扁平。失魂。

我深爱这首歌。诚然，我如此深爱，无需懂得一字一句。当我在 YouTube 上聆听时，我看到了江珊近期在中国节目中的演唱。

请听。江珊的嗓音依然优雅动人。然而，中国音乐——乃至我们所有东方音乐的现代化，似乎陷入了一种迷思。制作人只追寻模式，只采用西洋乐器，他们视莫扎特为“天才”的定义，认为其优于一切东方的旋律。于他们而言，将音乐西化，是一种文明之举。

他们眼中，音乐除却模式，别无他物。模式越精巧，音乐越上乘。这正是西方视角审视人类美学时，常抱有的算法式逻辑。

恭喜。如今，这种算法式的、失魂的音乐，确可被 AI 算法取代。但你永远无法用任何算法取代原版的《梦里水乡》。它源自人类的意识——而那被西方主流简化为大脑化学物质涌现属性的意识。

依此逻辑，我们无非是一套可被计算机模拟的数学关系。将一首标志性的中国歌曲剥除所有中国乐器，是对中国音乐灵魂的刺杀之举。这是一种模仿，正如如今几乎每一位东方音乐人所作所为，不论在中东还是中国。他们无法提供任何新物，唯有以西方的、扁平的、失魂的、算法式的模式，扼杀旧日之美。他们已是机器人，并且很快将被 AI 机器人取代。

纵有一千个莫扎特，每个拥有一千次生命，也无法企及《梦里水乡》的四分钟，或《溪行桃花源》的寥寥数分钟。那些艺术家与他们的音乐，将永世长存。

而那些现代的机器人乐师，其生命将如社交媒体上机器人账号所造的趋势一般，转瞬即逝。

过年快乐!

艾尔曼

You could listen to Jiang Shan’s song “Dreaming of My Homeland” (《梦里水乡》), without understanding a single word, and yet set your imagination free. While listening, you will travel to villages nestled between mountains, to rivers hugging the hills like a silken qiyao ruqun dress. You can smell the damp soil of unpaved village roads, see the memories held in the footprints saved within their earth, and watch smoke escape the roofs of old kitchens, twisting like a Chinese Water Sleeve Dance. You can see the morning mist resting on the necks of the mountains.

The bamboo flute rises above a Chinese rural village and lifts you toward the sun. The golden reflection of sunlight on the river fills your eyes with a magical happiness. Then, other birds accompany you with the voice of the guzheng, tingling your soul with a smooth pleasure—a pure, natural joy.

It captures the duty of the bird returning to its nest before sunset, the joy of a mother receiving her long-absent child.

Your mind can picture a feather in the hand of an ancient Chinese master, painting a watercolor (水墨画). You float with that feather, smoothly feeling the beautiful conclusion of the painting in its final stroke.

This is the soul of Chinese aesthetics, but in tones. There is no difference between Chinese music and Chinese landscape painting. If a piece of Chinese music does not evoke this feeling in you, does not paint the aesthetics of gardens, streams, mountains, sunsets, and floating clouds… of Nature itself… then it is not Chinese music. It is imported. Flat. Soulless.

I love this song. Honestly, I love it so much that I do not need to understand a word. While listening to it on YouTube, I found Jiang Shan singing it recently on a Chinese show.

Now listen. Jiang Shan still has that elegant, beautiful voice. But it seems the modernization of Chinese music—and all of our Eastern music—is trapped by a certain logic. Producers look for patterns, they use only Western instruments, they think Mozart is the definition of “genius,” superior to every Eastern melody. For them, westernizing your music is a civilized act.

They see nothing in music except patterns. The smarter the pattern, the better the music. This is precisely how the Western lens often views human aesthetics: as algorithmic.

Congratulations. Now, this algorithmic, soulless music can be replaced by an AI algorithm. But you will never replace the original “Dreaming of My Homeland” with any algorithm. It comes from human consciousness—a consciousness that much of the West dismisses as merely an emergent property of brain chemicals.

By that logic, we are nothing but a set of mathematical relationships that can be simulated in a computer. Taking an iconic Chinese song and stripping it of all Chinese instruments is an act of assassination against the soul of Chinese music. It is an act of mimicry, as every single Eastern musician seems to do nowadays, whether in the Middle East or in China. They have nothing new to offer except the killing of an old beauty, replacing it with Western, flat, soulless, algorithmic patterns. They are robots, and they will soon be replaced by AI robots.

A thousand Mozarts, each with a thousand lifetimes, could not come close to the four minutes of “Dreaming of My Homeland” or the few minutes of “Streamside Peach Blossom Spring” (《溪行桃花源》). Those artists and their music will live forever.

The modern robotic musicians will live and die like a trend created by a bot on a social media platform.

Happy chinese new year – Aiman
