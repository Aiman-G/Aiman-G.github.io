I have started to have angry responses to LLMs while vibe coding. 
Anger stems, as always, from expectations, the broken ones.

See, we see these new creatures of us as very capable and strong, they should be perfect.

But perfect in what? in understanding us, and what we want to achieve, what we think.

That is the poison of every relationship. 

Our relationship with these LLMs now is built on that expectation. 

We work with that expectation in mind, since we started delegating our thinking to these machines.

Anyway, if you agree that writing is thinking, then you might agree with me that: **coding is thinking** too.

Indeed, coding is writing.. it is thinking.

The idea that could shift your mind, that could grant you a new perspective on the problem you are solving, is no longer within your reach.

Because, when you vibe code, you are not thinking about the problem in such a deep way.

Thinking is one of the hardest tasks a human can do, our brains will always escape anything that could drain our energy.

So, we cannot pretend that while vibe coding, we can also think at the same deep level as we do while coding.

 **what is the solution**?

At this moment, the solution I see is to do the vibe coding in very small chunks of the code, very narrow.
So narrow that we do not lose the global view of the problem, nor do we miss a better idea that can come to our minds while coding.

To delegate very boring and very narrow tasks only. 

How to know these narrow tasks?
If you are a programmer, you already have the taste to do so.

If you are a new programmer, do not start vibe coding, do the programming, and learn the thinking. 


In conclusion:
Writing is thinking ---> coding is thinking. 
Vibe coding deprives you of the engagement that leads to clear thinking, and you may overlook great details.
